Google Calendar All-Day Expander

In Google Calendar's day and week views, the all-day events strip at the top has a fixed maximum height. When you have several all-day events on the same day, the extra ones are clipped and hidden behind the hourly grid below.

This extension gives you two ways to see them, from the toolbar popup:

  • Expand all-day area — removes the height cap so every all-day event is shown at once. Click again to restore the original layout.
  • Enable drag handle — adds a draggable grip on the border between the all-day strip and the hourly grid, so you can size the area to exactly what you want. Click again to remove the grip.

The two options are mutually exclusive — turning one on turns the other off — and each acts as a toggle you can switch back off. The change is purely visual: it only adjusts the height of the all-day strip and adds an optional resize grip.

Your last choice is saved on your device using Chrome's local extension storage and is re-applied automatically each time Google Calendar loads, so it survives a page reload. The extension runs only on calendar.google.com, keeps everything on your device, and sends no data anywhere.
